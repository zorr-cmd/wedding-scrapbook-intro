Plate I.  —  Untitled, Mt. Shasta, California, 2024.
From the series ‘The Brothers.’ Pigment print on cotton rag.

There is a quiet conviction in [GROOM]’s photographs that recalls the long-form ensemble work of August Sander or Mary Ellen Mark. For nearly a decade, working with a single recurring cast of eight men, he has constructed a visual record of contemporary American friendship — a subject most photographers consider unphotographable.

These pictures resist the easy categories of travel photography, ethnography, or family album. They are, instead, what they purport to be: durable evidence that a group of people decided, eleven times, that this moment was worth standing still for.

On the occasion of [GROOM]’s marriage to [BRIDE], we present this work in its entirety for the first time. The eleven plates that follow represent ten years of patient attention. — The Editors, MMXXVI
