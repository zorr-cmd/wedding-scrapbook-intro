“The brothers built smaller pyramids.”
— ANONYMOUS, MMXXII

It was decided, by no one in particular and yet by all eight, that the Great Pyramid of Giza required a smaller pyramid in its company. The boys removed their head coverings and replaced them with traditional keffiyehs purchased that morning from a vendor named Mohamed, who did not believe they had ever built a pyramid before.

They built the pyramid in three minutes and held it for seven. A photograph was taken. The base did not collapse, which surprised everyone, including Mohamed.

“It is a small pyramid,” Mohamed conceded, “but it is a pyramid.”

The author kept the keffiyeh.
