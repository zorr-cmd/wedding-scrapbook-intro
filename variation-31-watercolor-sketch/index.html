the boys — a watercolor diary

ten years on the road, with brushes that weren't supposed to be there, for [GROOM] on his wedding day.

mt. shasta — october.
the day we agreed to
do this one more time.

giza — october.
we built a pyramid in front
of the pyramid.
(he did not believe us)

the narrows — spring.
knee-deep, brushes ruined,
worth it.

i thought about giving him photographs. but the camera
was always too obvious — it stole the seconds
instead of holding them. so i bought a tin of paints,
a moleskine, and i sat. for ten years.

...continued — the rest of the boys.

painted on planes, in dorms, on boat decks, in canyons.
mostly with the water that was nearby.

la jolla — the brown
shirt summit.

cabo — land's end.
painted on the boat.

sedona — the red ones.
kept making them redder.

times square, 2 a.m.
tried to paint the neon.

the pile — golden hour.
laughing at no one.

sedona, take two.
[NAME 2]'s stone spiral.

the dorm year.
painted from memory.

every trip i kept telling them:
"one day i'll paint this."
one day became a decade.
then a wedding.

warriors of zion.
sticks raised, mythic.

the camera was too obvious.
the brush gave me an excuse
to sit still long enough
to remember.
— the route, more or less —
p.s. — [GROOM], i'm sorry the
wedding scene isn't painted yet.
i'll bring my brushes.
and the bad jokes.
and the rest of the boys.
