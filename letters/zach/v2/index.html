A Letter·June 6

Nepal.

I can’t believe this time has already come, but I’m so happy that it has. I still remember exactly where I was that late night when you called with the big news (unfortunately, the answer was “still at the office”). All I could think about the rest of the night was how happy, proud, and excited I was for you, and that hasn’t changed one bit.

There’s an incredible number of things I admire about you. Your endless passion for anything you do (film, Nerf guns, Lego YouTube videos). Your incredible force of will, your ability to set eyes on an unclimbable mountain, say fuck it, and somehow climb it (case in point, a certain upcoming artistic production). But the thing I admire most is your curiosity and desire to learn, about the world around you, and about everyone in it. I’ve never met anyone who can connect with people as quickly, genuinely, and deeply as you, and it’s a trait that I admire so deeply. You are just such a good person, and you deserve the best that life has to offer; I’m so glad you’re getting what you deserve.

All this to say, you are one of my best friends. These last 10 years have been a wild ride. Here’s to the next 50.

— Zach
