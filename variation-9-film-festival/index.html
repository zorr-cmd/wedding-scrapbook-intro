Programmer's Note
By the Selection Committee · 4,200 words abridged

When we began assembling this year's retrospective, the work of [GROOM] presented an unusual challenge for the selection committee. His oeuvre — eleven principal works, completed in collaboration with a rotating ensemble of seven brothers across a decade and four continents — defies easy categorization. Is it ethnography? Travelogue? Comedy of manners? We have settled, finally, on what feels truest: it is a slow-motion love letter, projected in eleven parts.

The films span the Pyramids of Giza (Geometry of Friendship, 2022), the slot canyons of Zion (Walking in Water, 2023), the alpenglow of Mt. Shasta (Aftermath of Light, 2024), and the neon-soaked corridors of dormitory life (Interior with Brothers, 2020). They are bound by a single recurring camera position — the wide ensemble shot, frame composed by self-timer, witnessed by the seven who made it.

The director's training in Yeşilçam-era staging is evident throughout: the patient master shot, the refusal to cut, the trust that the moment will arrive if only one waits for it. What distinguishes [GROOM] from his peers is not the equipment, nor the locations, but the unembarrassed sincerity with which he frames his subjects. They are not "characters." They are kardeşlerim. They are the work itself.

It is our privilege, on the occasion of his wedding, to present these works together for the first time. May this programme serve as the catalogue of record — and may every screening sell out.

— The Programming Committee, MMXXVI
